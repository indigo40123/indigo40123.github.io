I am a Physics Master student at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University(NYCU), studying experimental neutrino physics. Currently, I work on the Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory (JUNO) & Super-Kamiokande (Super-K) collaboration. More generally, I am interested in the rare neutrino events, such as MeV Dark Matter in Miliky way galaxy and Supernovae Relic Neutrino. I am also very passionate about physics education.

Outside of physics, I spend time writing, hiking, watching movies, cooking spagetties, and (most importantly) feeding my cat.

Before coming to NYCU, I did my undergraduate at National Taiwan Normal University. I graduated in June 2018 with a B.S. in Physics with certificated of physics teacher in senier high school. As an undergrad, I worked with Prof. Yi-Jehng Kuan on Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array cycle-4 data analysis research, aim to claim a detection of important prebiotically molecular - pyrimidine.
